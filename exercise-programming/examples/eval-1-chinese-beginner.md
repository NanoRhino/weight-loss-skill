# Test Case 1: Beginner · Commercial Gym · Fat Loss + Muscle Gain · 3 Days/Week

**User Input:**
> I want to start working out, help me create a training plan. I'm a complete beginner, never been to a gym. Male, 28, 175cm, 80kg. Goal is fat loss and muscle gain. Can train 3 days per week, 1 hour each. There's a commercial gym near my house with a good equipment selection. I prefer strength training and don't like running. I get slightly winded climbing 3-4 flights of stairs but can still talk. No injuries.

---

**Claude Response:**

Based on your profile, I've designed a 3-day full body training plan. Full body training is the most efficient for beginners — each muscle group gets stimulated 3 times per week, leading to faster progress.

> ⚠️ This training plan is for informational and educational purposes only. It is not a substitute for professional medical advice. Proper form is critical — consider asking a gym trainer to check your form during the first 1-2 weeks.

## Progression Plan

| Phase | Content |
|------|------|
| **Weeks 1-2** | Learn movements, RPE 6-7, no rush to add weight |
| **Weeks 3-4** | Progressive overload: if RPE ≤7 → add 2.5kg or do 1-2 more reps. Exercise upgrades: Goblet Squat → Barbell Squat, Lunges → Bulgarian Split Squat |
| **Week 5 (Deload)** | Only do 2 sets per exercise, same weight |
| **Week 6 onward** | Start a new cycle at a higher baseline |

Track your exercises, weights, and reps using a notes app — this is the key to progress.

## Reference

### Intensity Scale (RPE)

| RPE | Meaning |
|-----|------|
| 6 | Could do 4+ more reps — fairly easy |
| 7 | Could do 3 more reps — moderate |
| 8 | Could do 2 more reps — challenging |

For the first two weeks, aim for RPE 6-7 on all exercises. If you finish a set feeling like you "still have gas in the tank," you're on track.

### Starting Weight Guide

| Exercise Type | Suggested Starting Weight |
|---------|------------|
| Upper body push (bench press, shoulder press) | 6-10kg dumbbells each hand, or empty barbell (20kg) |
| Upper body pull (rows, pulldowns) | 8-12kg dumbbells each hand, or lightest machine setting |
| Lower body (squats, deadlifts) | 8-12kg dumbbell for goblet squat, or empty barbell |

If 10 reps still feel easy → increase weight next set; if you hit failure before 8 reps → too heavy, drop one increment.
