# Test Case 3: Female · Knee Meniscus Injury · Home Dumbbells + Yoga Mat · 2 Days/Week

**User Input:**
> I have a meniscus injury in my knee. The doctor said I can exercise but should avoid squats and running. I want to stay healthy and improve my fitness. Female, 45. Can train 2 days per week, 45 minutes each. Can only train at home with a pair of dumbbells and a yoga mat. I enjoy yoga and don't like high-intensity exercise. I get noticeably winded and need to pause after climbing 3 flights of stairs.

---

**Claude Response:**

I completely understand your situation. I've designed a safe and gentle home program for you. All exercises avoid squats and running, and incorporate the yoga you enjoy.

> ⚠️ This training plan is for reference only. You have a meniscus injury — **it is strongly recommended that you consult a physical therapist before starting** to confirm that the following exercises are appropriate for you. If you experience joint pain (not muscle soreness) in your knee during training, stop the exercise immediately.

### Tuesday: Full Body Training + Yoga Cooldown
Estimated time: ~45 minutes

Follow-along video:
[▶ Low impact full body dumbbell workout (no squats, no jumping)](https://www.youtube.com/results?search_query=low+impact+full+body+dumbbell+workout+no+squats+no+jumping+fitnessblender)

#### Warm-up (~5 minutes)

1. March in place for 2 minutes, relaxed pace
2. Cat-Cow ×10, follow your breath
3. Supine hip circles ×8 each side
4. Glute Bridge ×10 (warm-up, glute activation)

#### Main Training

**Exercise 1: Glute Bridge | RPE 6**
Knee-friendly, activates glutes
Set 1 ×12-15
Rest 60 seconds
Set 2 ×12-15
Rest 60 seconds
Set 3 ×12-15
Rest 90 seconds → move to next exercise

**Exercise 2: DB Romanian Deadlift | RPE 6-7**
Keep knees slightly bent and stationary, push hips back
Set 1 ×10-12
Rest 90 seconds
Set 2 ×10-12
Rest 90 seconds
Set 3 ×10-12
Rest 60 seconds → move to next exercise

**Exercise 3: Kneeling Push-up | RPE 6-7**
Do on yoga mat to protect knees
Set 1 ×8-10
Rest 60 seconds
Set 2 ×8-10
Rest 60 seconds
Set 3 ×8-10
Rest 60 seconds → move to next exercise

**Exercise 4: Single-Arm DB Row | RPE 6-7**
One hand on a chair (place chair against wall to prevent sliding)
Set 1 Left ×10 → Right ×10
Rest 60 seconds
Set 2 Left ×10 → Right ×10
Rest 60 seconds
Set 3 Left ×10 → Right ×10
Rest 60 seconds → move to next exercise

**Exercise 5: Straight-Leg Raise**
Gently strengthens the quadriceps, maintaining muscle strength around the knee
Set 1 Left leg ×10 → Right leg ×10
Rest 60 seconds
Set 2 Left leg ×10 → Right leg ×10

#### Yoga Cooldown (~12 minutes)

All poses below avoid positions that put pressure on the knee:

1. Cat-Cow ×8, gentle version
2. Knees to Chest — 1 minute
3. Supine Twist — left side 1 minute
4. Supine Twist — right side 1 minute
5. Reclined Figure-4 Stretch — left side 1 minute
   **Replaces Pigeon Pose**, much less knee pressure
6. Reclined Figure-4 Stretch — right side 1 minute
7. Supine hamstring stretch — use a towel looped around the foot, left side 1 minute
8. Supine hamstring stretch — right side 1 minute
9. Reclined Butterfly — 1 minute
10. Savasana — 3 minutes, close your eyes, breathe deeply

> If any pose causes knee discomfort, skip it.

## Progression Plan

| Phase | Details |
|------|------|
| **Weeks 1-2** | Light dumbbells, RPE 6, focus on learning the movements |
| **Weeks 3-4** | Feeling easy → add 2 reps per set, or slow down the movement (3-second lowering) |
| **Week 5 (Deload)** | Only do 2 sets per exercise, add more yoga cooldown |
| **Week 6 onward** | New cycle: single-leg glute bridge instead of double-leg, increase reps, or add slight weight |

**If your dumbbells are fixed weight and not adjustable**, that's perfectly fine:
- Increase reps (12→15→20)
- Slow down the tempo (3-second lowering, 2-second pause at the bottom)
- Progress to single-leg variations (double-leg glute bridge → single-leg glute bridge)
- Reduce rest between sets (60 seconds → 45 seconds)

## Important Reminders

- Muscle soreness (glutes or back of thighs) = normal
- **Joint pain (sharp knee pain, swelling) = not normal** → stop immediately, contact your doctor if it persists

---

## Reference

### Intensity Guide (RPE)

| RPE | Meaning |
|-----|------|
| 6 | Could do 4+ more reps — fairly easy |
| 7 | Could do 3 more reps — moderate |

Target RPE 6 for the first two weeks. If it feels "still very easy," you're on track.

### Starting Weight Suggestions

| Exercise Type | Suggested Starting Weight |
|---------|------------|
| Upper body push (shoulder press, push-ups) | 2-4kg each |
| Upper body pull (rows, flys) | 4-6kg each |
| Lower body (deadlifts, hip thrusts) | 4-8kg each |

If you can do 10 reps and it still feels easy → increase weight or reps next time; if you can't reach 8 reps → it's too heavy.
