# Test Case 4: Couch-to-5K · Running + Bodyweight + Flexibility

**User Input:**
> I want to run a 5K in three months — I've never run before. Female, 32 years old, 163 cm, 58 kg. My daily activity is just walking to and from work. I like outdoor activities but don't like going to the gym. No equipment — just my body and outdoor spaces. I get slightly winded climbing 4 flights of stairs but can still talk. No injuries. I can train 5 days per week, 30–45 minutes each session.

---

**Claude Response:**

Going from zero to 5K in 3 months is absolutely doable! I've designed a comprehensive plan with running as the core, supplemented by bodyweight strength and flexibility work to help you build running ability safely.

> ⚠️ This training plan is for reference only. Running is a high-impact activity — if you experience persistent joint pain during training (not normal muscle soreness), rest for a few days. If it's serious, see a doctor.

---

### Monday: Run/Walk Interval Training
Estimated duration: ~35 minutes

Follow-along reference:
[▶ Couch to 5K Run/Walk Intervals (Week 1)](https://www.youtube.com/results?search_query=couch+to+5k+week+1+run+walk+interval+beginner)

#### Warm-up (~5 minutes)

1. Brisk walk 3 minutes
2. Leg Swings (front/back) ×10 each side
3. Hip Circles ×8 each side
4. High Knees marching in place ×10 each side

#### Main Workout

**Run/Walk Intervals (Weeks 1–2 plan)**
Keep the entire session at a conversational intensity — when running, slightly winded but able to speak in full sentences

Jog 1 min → brisk walk 2 min, repeat 8 rounds (total ~24 minutes)

> When running, there's no need to go fast. "Being able to chat with a friend while running" is the correct pace. If you can't keep running, walk. Alternating between walking and running is the most scientific way to start.

#### Cool-down (~5 minutes)

1. Slow walk 2 minutes, let heart rate come down
2. Calf stretch 30 sec each side
3. Quad stretch 30 sec each side
4. Hamstring stretch 30 sec each side
5. Hip flexor lunge stretch 30 sec each side

### Saturday: Long Slow Run/Walk
Estimated duration: ~35–45 minutes

#### Warm-up (~5 minutes)

1. Brisk walk 3 minutes
2. Leg Swings (front/back) ×10 each side
3. High Knees ×10 each side

#### Main Workout

**Long Distance Run/Walk (Weeks 1–2 plan)**
Today's goal is distance and time, not speed. Keep the entire session easy.

Brisk walk 5 min (transition) → Jog 2 min / brisk walk 3 min, repeat 5 rounds (total ~25 min) → Brisk walk 5 min (cool-down walk)

> Don't worry about pace. If you feel "I could keep going," you're doing it right.

#### Cool-down (~5 minutes)

1. Slow walk 2 minutes
2. Calf stretch 30 sec each side
3. Quad stretch 30 sec each side
4. Hamstring stretch 30 sec each side
5. Full-body stretch 30 sec

---

### Sunday: Full Rest
- No exercise, recover well
- You can do 5–10 minutes of light stretching, but it's not required

## Progression Plan (12 Weeks → 5K)

| Week | Mon/Thu: Run/Walk Intervals | Saturday: Long Distance |
|------|---------------------------|------------------------|
| **Weeks 1–2** | Jog 1 min / walk 2 min × 8 rounds | Jog 2 / walk 3 × 5 rounds |
| **Weeks 3–4** | Jog 2 min / walk 1.5 min × 7 rounds | Jog 3 / walk 2 × 5 rounds |
| **Weeks 5–6** | Jog 3 min / walk 1 min × 6 rounds | Jog 5 / walk 1 × 4 rounds |
| **Weeks 7–8** | Jog 5 min / walk 1 min × 5 rounds | Continuous jog 15 min + walk 5 + jog 10 |
| **Weeks 9–10** | Continuous jog 20 min | Continuous jog 25 min |
| **Week 11** | Continuous jog 25 min | Continuous jog 30 min (~4–5 km) |
| **Week 12** | Easy jog 15 min × 2 (taper) | 5K! |

**Strength training (Tuesday) progression**: Starting week 5, add 1 set or 3–5 more reps per exercise. Starting week 9, you can add plyometric exercises (jump squats, jump lunges) to boost running explosiveness.

## Gear Recommendations

- **Running shoes**: The most important piece of gear. Visit a running specialty store to try them on — comfort and fit are what matter
- **Clothing**: Moisture-wicking fabrics. Skip cotton (heavy and uncomfortable once you sweat)
- **App**: Nike Run Club or Strava recommended for tracking distance and pace

## Appendix

### What Is the Right Running Intensity?

Use the "talk test" — no need to check heart rate:
- ✅ Can speak in full sentences while running → correct intensity
- ⚠️ Can only say a few words before needing to gasp → too fast, slow down
- ❌ Can't speak at all → walk for a bit, then run again

### FAQs

**"What if I can't keep running?"** — Walk. Alternating between walking and running is the scientific approach to getting started.

**"My calves are really sore / my shins hurt?"** — This could be shin splints, which are common for beginners. Reduce your running volume, do more calf stretches and calf raises, and make sure you're wearing proper running shoes. If the pain lasts more than a week, see a doctor.

**"How fast should I run?"** — As a beginner, every run should be a "jog." The correct speed = being able to chat with a friend while running.
